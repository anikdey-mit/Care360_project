General Safety Recommendations



Please read the instructions below and follow these in your day to day life. Also, look around you and your grandparent's house and try to follow safety instructions and make your house safer.




Adequate Lighting

  • Make sure there are adequate lighting in stairs,garage,walkways,at all doors in your grandparents house.
  • Replace any burnt-out light bulbs.
  • Try to install motion detection lighting inside and outside the home.
  • Install a nightlight in the bathroom. This will help your grandparents who may make repeated trips to the bathroom overnight. Install a nightlight or two on the route to the bathroom as well so that they can find their way.

Living room safety

  • Make sure the floors are slip resistant.
  • There should be no unobstructed path through the room (no clutter, cords, wires, baskets or other things to trip over).
  • Create a more open environment with no objects on the floor.
  • With your parents move large, heavy, or breakable decorative objects away from the edges of tables and off low hanging shelves in your grandparents house.

Bedroom Safety

  • Make sure to remove hazardous items,such as electric blankets and hot water bottles from your grandparent's bedroom.
  • Remove candles from the room.If left burning and unattended, candles can start a fire in the house.
  • Make sure of having a light at the entrance to the room of your grandparents and reachable from the bed.
  • Make sure having a telephone and assistive walking devices (cane, walker, wheelchair) within reach of your grandparents bed(within hand distance).

Kitchen Safety

  • Make sure gas access to the stove been controlled (knobs removed, lock on oven door, stove connected to hidden circuit breaker or gas valve).
  • Label all drawers and cabinets with safe objects in your grandparents house.
  • Make sure sharp knives and other dangerous implements been removed or locked up in your grandparents kitchen. Take your parents help in that.
  • Try to put rubberized water faucet covers for the kitchen sink. These can be easier to grip and turn and are color-coded: red for hot and blue for cold.

Bathroom Safety

  • Recommend you grandparents to replace bathtub with walk in models.
  • Put rubber mats in the your grandparents bathtub to prevent slipping.
  • Ask your parents to set the thermostat on the water heater no higher than 120° F to prevent accidental burns to your grandparents.
  • Make sure a shower or bath seat is present in your grandparents bathroom to allow them to direct the flow of water desired.

Shower Safety

  • Place a non skid bathmat in your grandparents washroom and bathtub..
  • Install grab bars in the shower for your grandparents.
  • Placing shampoo, soap and other toiletries where they can be reached easily by your grandparents possibly two - three feet above surface..

Laundry Safety

  • Make sure having a non-slip floor surface in your grandparents laundry room.
  • Mark all detergent containers in your grandparents laundry room..
  • Keep machine doors locked, even when the cycle is off.
  • Make sure that your loved one wears non-slip footwear or place them at the entry of laundry room..
  • Recommend your grandparents to use top loader washing machine.

Toilet Safety

  • Make sure sturdy grab bars at the toilet (or toilet arms and a raised seat) in your grandparents toilets are present.
  • Make sure toilet paper easily reachable from the toilet seat within a hand distance.
  • Help your grandparents to clean the tub regularly.
  • Replace the original toilet seat with a raised toilet seat with handlebars. Toilets should be between 17 and 19 inches in height. Seniors will find it easier to sit and stand.
